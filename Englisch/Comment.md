In the novel "Tell the Wolves I'm Home" by Carol Rifka Brunt, the author dives into the complexities of life and the profound impact of our choices by revealing us Junes thoughts about it. 

June sees life like  a sort of narrowing tunnel that gets narrower with every choice.(cf. ll. 10-11) It feels like she thinks we start with a lot of options, but as we make choices, those options get fewer. She's also wondering why people do things they don't like, like dealing with taxes. (cf. ll. 7) This shows she's looking for meaning in what we do. And when her dad talks about doing things for his family, June feels sad that someone would give up their own happiness for others.

Thinking of life like a narrowing tunnel could make June feel kind of stuck or limited. It's like she's looking for more meaning and happiness in what she does. Her empathy for her dad's sacrifices shows that she cares about how choices affect people emotionally.

These thoughts of June remind me of how teenagers often question things. They're trying to figure out who they are and what life means. June questioning why people do things they don't like is pretty common for teenagers who want to do their own thing: "I really wondered why people were always doing what they didn't like doing"(ll. ). Her empathy for others' feelings shows she's growing more aware of how choices impact people emotionally.

In conclusion, June's thoughts give us a lot to think about. She's not just talking about herself but also making us reflect on our own lives. Her ideas capture the challenges of growing up and searching for meaning in a world where choices seem to narrow down.
